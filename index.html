How it works

Block algorithmic feeds

Scrolless hides the infinite feeds on sites like Reddit, Instagram, YouTube, and more — right inside Safari.

Keep what matters

Allow-list specific pages like subreddits, YouTube subscriptions, or profiles so you can still browse intentionally.

No off switch

Want to undo a block? There's a 30-minute waiting period, stopping impulsive decisions to re-enable feeds.

Custom rules

Add your own platforms and define blocking & allow rules with simple URL patterns.

Works on iOS & Mac

Install once and get feed-blocking across all your Apple devices through Safari.

Pre-configured for 9+ sites

Out of the box support for Reddit, Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, Threads, and Bluesky.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 6, 2026

Overview

Scrolless is designed with privacy at its core. The extension operates entirely on your device and does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data.

Data collection

Scrolless does not collect any personal information. Your browsing activity, blocking rules, and app settings remain on your device and are never sent to any server.

Safari extension

The Safari extension only accesses web page content to apply your blocking and allow rules. It does not read, log, or transmit any page content or browsing history.

Analytics

Scrolless does not use any analytics, tracking, or crash-reporting services.

Third parties

No data is shared with third parties. The app has no server-side component and makes no network requests.
